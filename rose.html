About Roses

Rose shrubs are a popular choice for gardens, and the flowers are perfect for boquets and gifts. Roses have been cultivated for thousands of years for their hardiness and beauty, resulting in an expansive variety of types and colors. Interestingly, rose petals are edible- just be careful to not consume any pesticides.

Caring for Roses

Rose bushes require plenty of watering- at least twice weekly. Make sure the soil has good drainage though, and ensure the entire root zone is saturated. Plucking wilted flowers from rose bushes can promote more flowering and should be done at least once a week, but should be avoided at least a month before the first frost.

Planting Roses

When planting roses, wear thick gloves to protect yourself from the thorny stems. Dig a hole over a foot wide, and loosen the roots before planting. Once the rose bush is rooted, water it thoroughly. Do not overcrowd rose bushes; try to keep them at least a couple feet apart.
