About Fuchsias

Fuchsias are great at attracting hummingbirds, making them ideal for planting in hanging baskets. Fuchsias come in a variety of shades of red and pink, and can be either bushy or viny.

Caring for Fuchsias

Fuchsias naturally perform best in mild climates, but can alternatively be treated as a plant for cool seasons. They don't need very much sun, but should get about four hours daily, being kept in mostly shaded areas. Watering should be done daily for moist, but not oversaturated, soil. Fuchsias should be stored indoors in winter and watered once a month during this period.

Planting Fuchsias

Fuchsias should be planted in the spring, when night temperatures are consistently above forty degrees. They perfer somewhat moist soil, but their pots should allow most water to drain out. In hotter climates, plant fuchsias in areas with speckled shade and fertilize about every two weeks.
